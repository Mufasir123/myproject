Tata Altroz Verdict

The Tata Altroz is an extremely well-rounded premium hatchback that will appeal to an even wider range of audiences due to the new turbo petrol engine from the Nexon. Don’t get us wrong, the performance from the 1.2-litre three-cylinder petrol engine was just about adequate for city use, but if you needed anything more from it, it just fell short.

Variants:

The Tata Altroz is available in six variants including XE, XE+, XM+, XT, XZ, and XZ+.

Market introduction:

The Tata Altroz was launched in India on 22 January, 2020.

Engine and specification:

Engine options on the Tata Altroz include a 1.2-litre NA petrol engine, a 1.2-litre turbo-petrol engine, and a 1.5-litre diesel engine. A five-speed manual unit is standard while a DCA transmission is available only with the 1.2 NA motor.

Exterior design:

Exterior highlights of the Tata Altroz include projector headlamps, LED DRLs, fog lights, a wide air dam, 16-inch dual-tone alloy wheels, C-pillar mounted rear door handles, wraparound LED tail lights, a large black insert for the tail gate, an integrated spoiler, rear wiper and washer, and a rear bumper-mounted number plate recess.

Interior and features:

Inside, the Tata Altroz comes equipped with features including a seven-inch touchscreen infotainment system with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, a flat-bottom steering wheel, a seven-inch TFT digital instrument console, mood lighting, cooled glove-box, steering-mounted controls, iRA connected car technology, drive modes, engine start-stop button, automatic climate control, rear AC vents, height-adjustable driver seat, and a front sliding arm-rest with storage.

Colours:

The Tata Altroz is offered in seven colours such as Harbour Blue, Downtown Red, Opera Blue, Arcade Grey, Avenue White, High Street Gold, and Dark edition.

Seating capacity:

The Tata Altroz has a seating capacity of five occupants.

Rivals:

The Tata Altroz rivals the Maruti Suzuki Baleno, Hyundai i20, Honda Jazz, and the Volkswagen Polo.
